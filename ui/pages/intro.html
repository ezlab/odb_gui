The Hierarchical Catalog of Orthologs   v8

Orthology is the cornerstone of comparative genomics and gene function prediction. OrthoDB aims to identify groups of orthologous genes across many available genomes that presumably originated from a single gene of the last common ancestor (LCA) of the species selected. Applying this concept to the hierarchy of LCAs along species phylogeny results in multiple levels of orthology. The more closely related the species, the more finely-resolved orthologous relations.


Examples of how you can query OrthoDB:   Cytochrome P450,  protease | peptidase,  kinase -serine,  FBgn0036816,  GO:0006950,  immune response,  stress response,  breast cancer,  diabetes.
