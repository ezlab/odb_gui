Search Parameters

Text Search

Enter a gene name, identifier, annotation keyword, phenotype, etc.

  • OrthoDB can be queried using relevant identifiers of proteins, genes, OrthoDB orthologous groups (EOG...), InterPro domains (IPR...), or Gene Ontology terms (GO:...), as well as with keywords associated with protein annotations.
    • Identifiers: UniProtKB, Ensembl, EntrezGene, KEGG, UniGene, GenBank, RefSeq, InterPro, Gene Ontology, AphidBase, BeetleBase, FlyBase, Hymenoptera Genome Database, LepBase, SilkDB, VectorBase, wFleaBase, Mouse Genome Informatics, Saccharomyces Genome Database, etc. e.g. 'P38903', 'CG10753', 'IPR001163'
    • Keyword annotations in UniProtKB and Ensembl: Protein names, gene names, etc. e.g. "Probable small nuclear ribonucleoprotein Sm D1"
    • Keyword phenotypes: For Homo sapiens, Mus musculus, Danio rerio, Caenorhabditis elegans, Drosophila melanogaster, Saccharomyces cerevisiae and Escherichia coli, you can search the database using phenotype keywords.
      • Homo sapiens: Human disease data from OMIM (Online Mendelian Inheritance in Man) e.g. "Diabetes mellitus"
      • Mus musculus: High-level Mammalian Phenotype Terms from MGI (Mouse Genome Informatics) e.g. phenotype: "immune system"
      • Danio rerio: Zebrafish phenotype data from ZFIN (The Zebrafish Model Organism Database) e.g. "dead"
      • Caenorhabditis elegans: Phenotypic data from WormBase e.g. "life span variant"
      • Drosophila melanogaster: Data from FlyBase for phenotypic classes containing keywords "lethal", "sterile", and "defective", e.g. "neurophysiology defective"
      • Saccharomyces cerevisiae: Phenotypic data from SGD (Saccharomyces Genome Database) e.g. 'inviable'
      • Escherichia coli: Phenotypic data from EcoGene and essentiality data from DEG (Database of Essential Genes)
  • Logical operator NOT use '-' or '!', e.g. 'kinase -serine' or 'kinase !tyrosine'
  • Logical operator OR use '|', e.g. 'protease | peptidase'.
  • Logical operator AND is implicit, i.e. 'sodium transporter' actually means 'sodium AND transporter' (not quoted phrases).
  • Use quotes to match a phrase literally, e.g. "Cytochrome P450".
  • Take advantage of the autocomplete lookup feature that offers keyword or identifier suggestions for your search.
  • Click the 'Submit' button (or return key) to execute the query.
  • The OrthoDB Search Engine is powered by Sphinx.

Sequence Search

Retrieve orthologous groups by homology to your query protein sequence.

  • Paste your protein sequence in any format (e.g. FASTA), only one sequence will be considered at a time, and click the 'Submit' button to execute the query.
  • Orthologous Groups with members that have significant matches to your query sequence will be returned.
  • The OrthoDB sequence search is powered by a modified version of RAPSearch.

Phyloprofile Filters

Filtering by phylogenetic presence and/or gene copy-number.

  • You can filter the list of Orthologous Groups returned to require them to have members in all species of the selected taxonomic node, or a fraction of them, e.g. present in all, in >90% or >80% of the species.
  • You can filter the list of Orthologous Groups returned to require them to have only single-copy orthologs in all species of the selected taxonomic node, or a fraction of them, e.g. single-copy in all, in >90% or >80% of the species.
  • You can combine any presence filter with any copy-number filter to refine your results, e.g. present in >90% AND single-copy in >80% of species.

Species Selection

Tailor your search by using the expandable species tree to select a radiation point or particular sets of species.

  • The expandable species tree is derived from the official NCBI Taxonomy.
  • OrthoDB Orthologous Groups are delineated at each major radiation node of the species phylogeny, with separate root nodes for Bacteria, Metazoa, Fungi, and Plants.
  • This hierarchy of Orthologous Groups means that considering many distantly-related species delineates fewer, more general (inclusive) orthologous groups containing all the descendants of the ancestral gene, while examining only sets of more closely-related species produces many fine-grained orthologous groups of mostly one-to-one relations.
  • Expand or collapse any node on the tree by clicking on the filled arrows or node names.
  • Select all species at a node by clicking on the unfilled box next to the node name, or select specific species by clicking on the unfilled box next to the species name.
  • The appropriate node to be searched (most recent common ancestor of all selected species) and the list of selected species to display is automatically updated based on your species and/or node selections.
  • You may also add species to the list of selected species to display by typing the species name in the search box and selecting from the autocompleted options.
  • As you add or remove species from the expandable species tree, the 'Species to display' box above it will automatically update to reflect your selections.

Species to display

Filter your results to display only the species of interest.

  • You may choose to view reference species only at a particular node by clicking the option in the 'Species to display' box, and then 'Submit'. These are selected to include species that are considered model species or are generally better studied.
  • If you have chosen to view only a subset of species, you may easily toggle back and forth with the full species view while browsing the 'Orthologs by organism' table using the 'Selected species only' checkbox.
  • You may reset your species selection using the 'Clear all' option from the 'Species to display' box, and then 'Submit'.

Results

Functional descriptions

Summary information of gene functional annotations.

  • Gene Ontology and InterPro attributes associated with individual member proteins provide a general description for the Orthologous Group as a whole.
  • These are summed over the member proteins to indicate the most frequently occurring attributes associated with the Orthologous Group.
  • Gene Ontology and InterPro identifiers are linked to their respective sources to access further detailed information describing the Terms and Domains.
  • Functional Categories are provided based on mapping to COG-defined broad gene functional categories.

Evolutionary descriptions

Summary information of the orthologous group’s evolutionary profile.

  • Phyletic Profile: a summary of the ortholog presence (from universal to species-specific) and copy-numbers (single/multi-copy counts).
  • Evolutionary Rate: Orthologous groups that exhibit appreciably higher or lower levels of sequence divergence are highlighted through quantification of the relative divergence among their member genes. These are computed for each orthologous group as the average of inter-species identities normalized to the average identity of all inter-species best reciprocal hits, computed from pairwise Smith-Waterman alignments of protein sequences. The relative rate is indicated by the position of the black star along the scale of slow-blue to fast-red rates.
  • Gene Architecture: The table shows mean, median, and standard deviation values of protein lengths and exon counts for each orthologous group, effectively describing a 'consensus' gene architecture (for those genes with available data).

Orthologs by organism

Orthologs listed per species with associated annotations.

  • The table view presents available information for each gene including gene/protein ID, UniProt ID, short description, number of amino acids (AAs), number of exons, and associated InterPro domains.
  • If additional information is available for a given gene a double-arrow icon can be clicked to view an expanded association annotation list with links to source databases.
  • Available (i.e. those mapped from UniProt) InterPro attributes are displayed for each protein member ordered from the N to C terminus.
  • To reduce redundancy, where a protein exhibits two or more domains related as child=>parent, only the child attribute is displayed, while all are searchable.
  • Click on the grey magnifying glass icon to query OrthoDB for groups containing proteins with the same domains.
  • To search for specific domain architectures, enter an ordered list of InterPro identifiers separated with only commas into the 'Text Search' box, e.g. 'IPR000253,IPR000719' Forkhead-domain, Kinase-domain
  • Gene synonyms and recorded phenotypes are integrated in OrthoDB for Human (from OMIM: Online Mendelian Inheritance in Man) and selected model organisms (C. elegans: WormBase, D. melanogaster: FlyBase, D. rerio: ZFIN The Zebrafish Model Organism Database, E. coli: EcoGene, M. musculus: MGI Mouse Genome Informatics, and S. cerevisiae: SGD Saccharomyces Genome Database).
  • Genes with synonyms or phenotypes are linked to their respective source databases.
  • For the length (AAs) and exon counts (Exons) listed for each gene, the exclamation mark (!) indicates differences from consensus (left: shorter, right: longer, !: 1 stdev, !!: 2 stdev).
  • As some species are included for complete orthology clustering while others are later mapped to defined clusters, those species included only through mapping are indicated with a grey M icon.

Sibling groups

Related orthologous groups at the same species radiation.

  • Relations among Orthologous Groups are defined according to their common InterPro domain annotations.
  • The top 5 groups are listed with their percentage overlap in terms of common InterPro domains, and the complete list of related groups may be retrieved by clicking the 'Show all siblings' link.

Export Results

Get All Fasta / View Fasta

Displays text view of search result protein sequences in Fasta format.

  • Get All Fasta = all proteins from your selected species from all orthologous groups that match your search.
  • View Fasta = all proteins from your selected species from the orthologous group you are viewing.
  • Group ID, gene, organism, and other useful details are contained in the header of each sequence. This information can be saved as a file by right-clicking on the link followed by "save link as...".

Get All as Tab Delimited / View Tab Delimited

Displays tab delimited text view of results.

  • Get All as Tab Delimited = all proteins from your selected species from all orthologous groups that match your search.
  • View Tab Delimited = all proteins from your selected species from the orthologous group you are viewing.
  • Group ID, taxonomic level, and protein IDs are listed for each sequence. This information can be saved as a file by right-clicking on the link followed by "save link as...".
