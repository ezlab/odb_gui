Search Parameters

Text Search

Enter a gene name, annotation keyword, phenotype, etc.

  • The database is can be queried using relevant identifiers of proteins, genes, orthologous groups, InterPro domains, or Gene Ontology terms, as well as with keywords associated with protein annotations.
    • Identifiers: UniProtKB, Ensembl, AphidBase, BeetleBase, FlyBase, Hymenoptera Genome Database, SilkDB, VectorBase, wFleaBase, Mouse Genome Informatics, Saccharomyces Genome Database, GenBank, RefSeq, InterPro, Gene Ontology, etc. E.g. 'P38903', 'CG10753', 'IPR001163'
    • Keyword annotations in UniProtKB and Ensembl: Protein names, gene names, etc. E.g. "Probable small nuclear ribonucleoprotein Sm D1"
    • Keyword phenotypes: For Homo sapiens, Mus musculus, Danio rerio, Caenorhabditis elegans, Drosophila melanogaster, Saccharomyces cerevisiae and Escherichia coli, you can search the database using phenotype keywords.
      • Homo sapiens: Human disease data from OMIM (Online Mendelian Inheritance in Man) e.g. "Diabetes mellitus"
      • Mus musculus: High-level Mammalian Phenotype Terms from MGI (Mouse Genome Informatics) e.g. phenotype: "immune system"
      • Danio rerio: Zebrafish phenotype data from ZFIN (The Zebrafish Model Organism Database) e.g. "dead"
      • Caenorhabditis elegans: Phenotypic data from WormBase e.g. "life span variant"
      • Drosophila melanogaster: Data from FlyBase for phenotypic classes containing keywords "lethal", "sterile", and "defective", e.g. "neurophysiology defective"
      • Saccharomyces cerevisiae: Phenotypic data from SGD (Saccharomyces Genome Database) e.g. 'inviable'
      • Escherichia coli: Phenotypic data from EcoGene and essentiality data from DEG (Database of Essential Genes)
  • Logical operator NOT use '-' or '!', e.g. 'kinase -serine' or 'kinase !tyrosine'
  • Logical operator OR use '|', e.g. 'protease | peptidase'.
  • Logical operator AND is implicit, i.e. 'sodium transporter' actually means 'sodium AND transporter' (not quoted phrases).
  • Use quotes to match a phrase literally (e.g. "Cytochrome P450").
  • Click the 'Text Search' button to execute the query.
  • The OrthoDB Search Engine is powered by Sphinx.

Sequence Search

Retrieve orthologous groups by homology to your query sequence.

  • Paste your sequence in any format, only one sequence is considered. The top hits from different species are returned.The Orthologous Group of the top hit will be displayed (which may also contain some or all of the other top hits).
  • If the top hit is not classified into an Orthologous Group, the other top hits may be used to query OrthoDB simply by clicking the linked protein identifier.

Phyloprofile Filters

Filtering by phylogenetic presence and/or copy-number variations.

  • You can require the orthologous groups to have members in all species of the selected taxonomic node, or a fraction of them (e.g. present in all, in 90% or 80% of the selected species).
  • Retrieve orthologous groups matching specific copy-number profiles (e.g. all single-copy or all multiple-copy).

Species Tree

Choose the radiation point or a particular set of species by clicking the buttons on the tree.

  • The phylogeny-defined hierarchy of orthologous groups allows you to select your required radiation point by clicking on the nodes of the species tree.
  • Your query will be restricted to searching all descendants of the currently selected root node marked in red.
  • Considering many distantly related species delineates fewer, more general (inclusive) orthologous groups containing all the descendants of the ancestral gene, while examining more closely related species produces many fine-grained orthologous groups of mostly one-to-one relations.
  • Metazoan Orthologous Groups are computed at the root node only, select the Vertebrate or Arthropod icons to browse these trees.
  • Bacteria Orthologous Groups are computed at the root node and the nodes of 11 major bacteria clades.

Functional Category

  • Gene Ontology and InterPro attributes associated with individual member proteins provide a general description for the Orthologous Group as a whole.
  • These are summed over all member proteins to indicate the most frequently occurring attributes associated with the Orthologous Group.
  • Gene Ontology and InterPro identifiers are linked to their respective sources to access further detailed information describing the Terms and Domains.
  • Available InterPro attributes are displayed for each protein member ordered from the N to C terminus.
  • To reduce redundancy, where a protein exhibits two or more domains related as child=>parent, only the child attribute is displayed, while all are searchable.
  • Click on the grey play button to query OrthoDB for groups containing proteins with the same domains.
  • To search for specific domain architectures, enter an ordered list of InterPro identifiers separated with only commas into the 'Text Search' box, e.g. 'IPR000253,IPR000719' Forkhead-domain, Kinase-domain
  • Gene synonyms and recorded phenotypes are integrated in OrthoDB for Human (from OMIM: Online Mendelian Inheritance in Man) and selected model organisms (C. elegans: WormBase, D. melanogaster: FlyBase, D. rerio: ZFIN The Zebrafish Model Organism Database, E. coli: EcoGene, M. musculus: MGI Mouse Genome Informatics, and S. cerevisiae: SGD Saccharomyces Genome Database).
  • Genes with synonyms or phenotypes are linked to their respective source databases.

Evolutionary Rate

  • Orthologous groups that exhibit appreciably higher or lower levels of sequence divergence are highlighted through quantification of the relative divergence among their member genes. These are computed for each orthologous group as the average of inter-species identities normalized to the average identity of all inter-species best reciprocal hits, computed from pairwise Smith-Waterman alignments of protein sequences.
  • The relative rate is indicated by the position of the black cell along the scale of slow-blue to fast-red rates, the slowest are less than ~half and the fastest are more than ~double the average rate:

Sibling Groups

  • This is a metric to measure similarity between groups, e.g. the sequence uniqueness of the orthologs. Orthologous groups presumably represent the gene content of a particular ancestral lineage, some of which may have originated by earlier gene duplications (i.e. paralogous genes) or they can share only a duplicated fragment, e.g. evolutionary mobile domain.
  • Relations among Orthologous Groups are defined according to the average minimum distances from all-against-all Smith-Waterman e-values between all members of an orthologous group to all members in other groups.
  • These Sibling Groups are defined at each level of the phylogeny-defined hierarchy, i.e. they are considered as sibling groups (rather than parent or child groups which would correspond to moving up or down a level in the hierarchy).
  • The top 5 groups are listed with their average and minimum e-values, and the complete list of related groups may be retrieved by clicking the 'view all' link.

Gene Architecture

  • The table Gene Architecture shows mean, median, and standard deviation values of protein lengths and exon counts for each orthologous group, effectively describing a 'consensus' gene architecture.
  • Length (AAs) and exon counts (Exons) are listed for each gene.
  • ! indicates difference from consensus (left: shorter, right: longer, !: 1 stdev, !!: 2 stdev).

Evolutionary Descriptions

  • We provide orthology classifications for genes from species with newly sequenced genomes mapped to existing orthologous groups.
  • You can browse the mapped orthologs for new species with publically available gene sets.
  • Web interfaces with restricted access provide the same functionality for private gene sets from organisms with recently-sequenced genomes.

Summary Panel

Get All Fasta

Displays text view of all search result sequences in Fasta format.

Group ID, gene, organism, and other useful details are contained in the header of each sequence. This information can be saved as a file by right-clicking on the link followed by "save link as...".

Get All as Tab Delimited

Displays tab-delimited text view of all sequences in results.

Group ID, taxonomic level, and protein IDs are listed for each sequence. This information can be saved as a file by right-clicking on the link followed by "save link as...".
